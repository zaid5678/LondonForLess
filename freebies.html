Free Museums

World-class collections, always free. These institutions cost nothing to enter and everything not to visit.

Free Museums

The British Museum

Eight million objects. Two million years of human history. The Rosetta Stone, Egyptian mummies, the Elgin Marbles — all of it free, all year round. Walk straight in, no ticket, no time limit. One of the genuinely great things about London.

📍 Great Russell St, WC1B 3DG 🕙 Daily 10am–5:30pm

Free Museums

Tate Modern

The world's most visited modern art museum — Warhol, Dali, Rothko, Basquiat. The permanent collection is free every single day. The building itself (a converted power station on the Thames) is worth the trip alone. Late nights on select Fridays hit different.

📍 Bankside, SE1 9TG 🕙 Sun–Thu 10am–6pm · Fri–Sat 10am–10pm
Plan Your Visit →
Free Museums

Victoria & Albert Museum

270 galleries of decorative arts and design spanning 5,000 years. The fashion collection alone is worth the trip. Free permanent collection always, plus blockbuster paid exhibitions. The café and garden courtyard are some of the most beautiful spaces in London.

📍 Cromwell Rd, SW7 2RL 🕙 Daily 10am–5:45pm · Fri to 10pm
